Understanding the Crisis

Our Ocean Needs Help

Human activities are putting enormous pressure on marine ecosystems. Here's what's happening beneath the waves.

🎣

Overfishing

Over 30% of fish populations are overfished. Industrial fishing removes fish faster than they can reproduce, disrupting the entire food chain.

90% of large predatory fish are gone
🌡️

Ocean Warming

The ocean absorbs 90% of Earth's excess heat. Warmer waters cause coral bleaching, forcing marine life to migrate or perish.

1.1°C average temperature rise
🗑️

Plastic Pollution

8 million tons of plastic enter our oceans yearly. Marine animals mistake plastic for food, causing injury and death.

5.25T plastic pieces in ocean
🧪

Ocean Acidification

CO2 absorption makes seawater more acidic, dissolving shells and skeletons of shellfish, corals, and plankton.

30% more acidic since 1800s
🧊

Think of the Ocean Like a Giant Refrigerator

If you keep taking food out without letting it refill, eventually it becomes empty. The ocean works the same way — we need to give marine life time to recover and maintain balance.

How You Can Help

Small actions add up to big changes. Here are practical ways you can become an ocean guardian in your daily life.

01

Choose Sustainable Seafood

Look for MSC (Marine Stewardship Council) certification. Use apps like Seafood Watch to make informed choices at restaurants and grocery stores.

02

Reduce Plastic Use

Carry reusable bags, bottles, and containers. Say no to single-use plastics like straws, utensils, and packaging whenever possible.

03

Save Water & Energy

Less energy use means less CO2, which means less ocean acidification. Shorter showers and turning off lights all make a difference.

04

Spread Awareness

Share what you've learned with friends and family. The more people understand ocean issues, the more collective action we can take.
